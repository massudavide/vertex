University of Aberdeen

The University of Aberdeen has been named as Scottish University of the Year in the Times and Sunday Times Good University Guide 2019.

Founded in 1495, the University of Aberdeen is Scotland's third oldest and the UK's fifth oldest university. Ranked among the top 160 universities in the world, Aberdeen offers hundreds of Bachelors and Masters degrees across all academic disciplines.

Additional information

Tuition fees at the University of Aberdeen

Students who are citizens of the EU/EEA:

The average tuition fees for students from the EU/EEA are 3,200 GBP per year, ranging from 500 GBP to 17,100 GBP.

Students who are citizens from outside the EU/EEA:

The average tuition fees for students from outside the EU/EEA are 17,300 GBP per year, ranging from 800 GBP to 32,400 GBP.

Tuition fees may vary for each study programme.

English requirements

When you apply to the University of Aberdeen, you will need to prove your English proficiency.

  • This university accepts IELTS. The general minimum IELTS score is 6.0 overall for Bachelors degrees and 6.5 overall for Masters degrees.
  • This university accepts TOEFL iBT. The general minimum TOEFL score is 78 overall for Bachelors degrees and 90 overall for Masters degrees.
  • This university accepts Cambridge English certificates (CAE/CPE). The general minimum score is 169 overall for Bachelors degrees and 176 overall for Masters degrees.
  • This university accepts the Pearson Test of English (PTE). The general minimum score is 54 overall for Bachelors degrees and 62 overall for Masters degrees.

Please note that the required English scores for admission may vary for certain programmes.

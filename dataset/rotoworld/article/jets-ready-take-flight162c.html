The Week Ahead

Jets ready to take flight

by Michael Finewax
January 15, 2021, 11:44 am ET
Updated On: January 15, 2021, 11:44 am ET
Print

Well, we are two nights into the 2020-21 schedule and there has been a postponement, although we all knew about that last Friday.

You can really tell there were only 10 days of practice for most teams as well as no pre-season games but by the time the end of Week 2, teams should be a lot better, as well as the pace of the game.

Happy Birthday to my brother Larry!

Let’s get to it!

WEEK OF JANUARY 18-24

5 Games – Winnipeg

4 Games – Anaheim, Arizona, Buffalo, Carolina, Colorado, Columbus, Detroit, Edmonton, Los Angeles, Minnesota, Montreal, Nashville, Philadelphia, San Jose, St. Louis, Toronto, Vancouver, Vegas

3 Games – Boston, Chicago, Dallas, Florida, New Jersey, New York Islanders, New York Rangers, Ottawa, Pittsburgh, Tampa Bay, Washington

2 Games - Calgary

Anaheim, Arizona and St. Louis play four games at home.

Buffalo, Colorado, Montreal, San Jose and Vegas play four games on the road.

Pittsburgh, Toronto and Vancouver play three games at home.

Edmonton and Winnipeg play three games on the road.

INJURIES

Seventeen Dallas Stars have COVID-19! (Click here to see the rest of the injury list.)

ANAHEIM

Games this week

vs. Minnesota, vs. Minnesota, vs. Colorado, vs. Colorado

Tired: None

Rested: vs. Colorado (Friday)

Notes

The Ducks lost their opener 5-2 to Vegas with John Gibson allowing four goals on 28 shots…Max Comtois got off to a good start, scoring both goals for Anaheim…It was a bit of a shocker to see Trevor Zegras sent to San Diego of the AHL, at least to start the season. He was the best player at the World Junior Championships (winning the MVP award), picking up a tournament-leading 18 points in just seven games, leading the USA to gold…Jamie Drysdale also had the same fate as the first-round pick in 2020 and sixth overall, heads down to San Diego…Defenseman Brendan Guhle is out six weeks with a sprained MCL.

ARIZONA

Games this week

vs. Vegas, vs. Vegas, vs. Vegas, vs. Vegas

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Coyotes play all four games at home against Vegas and they won’t see Vegas again in Glendale unless they play each other in the post-season…The Coyotes got off to a tough start, losing 4-3 in a shootout to San Jose…Conor Garland led the way with a goal and an assist…Lawson Crouse played on Thursday as he was healthy after being on the NHL’s COVID Protocol Related Absence list the day before…While it’s not fantasy-related, Shane Doan was named the Coyotes Chief Hockey Development Officer and help out owner Alex Meruelo and GM Bill Armstrong…Antti Raanta practiced with the Coyotes for the first time this season on Sunday and should get around 25 starts this season with Darcy Kuemper picking up 30.    

BOSTON

Games this week

@ New York Islanders, vs. Philadelphia, vs. Philadelphia

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Bruins won their first game of the season Thursday but it took a shootout to do so as they upended the New Jersey Devils…Brad Marchand was questionable heading into Thursday night’s opener but played and had a power play goal as well as an assist…Craig Smith is out with a lower-body injury…Karson Kuhlman is also out of action, but he is in COVID-19 protocol…David Pastrnak is skating with his teammates after undergoing off-season surgery on his hip. He was expected to be out until mid-February but is ahead of schedule…The Bruins and Flyers will play an outdoor game in Lake Tahoe on February 21…Jack Studnicka opened the season on the first line with Marchand and Patrice Bergeron.

BUFFALO

Games this week

@ Philadelphia, @ Philadelphia, @ Washington, @ Washington

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Sabres started Carter Hutton in their opening game against Washington over Linus Ullmark as they were hoping Hutton could get off to the same 6-0 start he did last season before faltering. Well he was off the mark, giving up five goals on 27 shots in a  6-4 loss…Taylor Hall had a power play goal and an assist in his Buffalo debut but it wasn’t as good for newcomer Eric Staal as the veteran center left the game after taking a stick to the head courtesy of Nic Dowd…Tage Thompson continues to see first line time with Jack Eichel and Hall and picked up an assist Thursday…Jack Quinn was assigned to the taxi squad while Dylan Cozens saw third line duty versus the Capitals and picked up an assist.

CALGARY

Games this week

vs. Vancouver, vs. Toronto

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Flames are the only team to play twice this week…Jacob Markstrom gave up four goals on 34 shots in an overtime loss to Winnipeg after being staked to a 3-1 led in the first period… David Rittich missed Thursday’s game due to a family issue as the Flames were forced to recall Louis Domingue from the taxi squad to back up Markstrom…Derek Ryan found himself on waivers Monday but passed through and played 10:43 Thursday…Elias Lindholm had a goal and an assist Thursday…Dominik Simon was on the first line Saturday in practice but found himself in the press box Thursday.

CAROLINA

Games this week

@ Nashville, @ Nashville, vs. Florida, vs. Florida

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Hurricanes play four games including three games in four nights. Petr Mrazek and James Reimer will likely share the net this week…Mrazek picked up the opening assignment against Detroit and got the shutout, albeit, having to make only 14 saves…The Hurricanes picked up some insurance on the blueline, dealing Clark Bishop to Ottawa for defenseman Maxime Lajoie…They also claimed Anton Forsberg off waivers and then waived their perceived goalie of the future Alex Nedeljkovic…Nino Niederreiter saw first line time with Sebastian Aho and Teuvo Teravainen as well as second line power play duty. He opened the scoring for the Canes.

CHICAGO

Games this week

@ Florida, vs. Detroit, vs. Detroit

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Blackhawks finish out in Florida before returning to Chicago for their home opener against Detroit…The Blackhawks gave Malcolm Subban the first start of the season but expect to see Collin Delia and Kevin Lankinen also receive playing time between the pipes this season…Chicago placed Jonathan Toews (undisclosed), Alexander Nylander (knee) and Kirby Dach (broken wrist) on the long-term injured list…Dylan Strome picked up the only goal in a 5-1 loss to Tampa Bay in the opener…Brent Seabrook has a lower-back injury and is hoping to resume skating soon…Carl Soderberg missed the opener due to quarantine but should be in the lineup this week, if not sooner.  

COLORADO

Games this week

@ Los Angeles, @ Los Angeles, @ Anaheim, @ Anaheim

Tired: @ Anaheim (Thursday)

Rested: None

Notes

The Avalanche are on the road for all four games this week but at least they remain in the LA area…Colorado plays three games in four nights so expect to see Philipp Grubauer play in two of the three games with Pavel Francouz seeing action in the other one…Gabriel Landeskog was back on the top line in practice Thursday with Nathan MacKinnon and Mikko Rantanen, while Andre Burakovsky was downgraded to the second unit with Nazem Kadri and Brandon Saad, this despite Burakovsky scoring the Avs lone goal Wednesday in a 4-1 loss to the Blues…Erik Johnson is out after testing positive for COVID-19…Defenseman Bowen Byram, the Avs first pick and fourth overall in 2019, will join the team Saturday after quarantining seven days following the WJC where he starred for Canada.

COLUMBUS

Games this week

@ Detroit, @ Detroit, vs. Tampa Bay, vs. Tampa Bay  

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Blue Jackets play four games this week including three in four nights. Expect Joonas Korpisalo and Elvis Merzlikins will share the net…Cam Atkinson was on the second line to start the opener against Nashville with Nick Foligno and Max Domi but ended the game on the third unit in only 14:02 of action…Mikko Koivu is in COVID-19 protocol and is currently unable to play or practice…Pierre-Luc Dubois continues to request a trade out of Columbus and actually spoke to his teammates this week about leaving. We shall see if GM Jarmo Kekalainen grants his request…Domi is looking to shoot the puck more this season even though he thinks of himself as a ‘pass-first’ player.  

DALLAS

Games this week

@ Tampa Bay, vs. Nashville, vs. Nashville

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

It was a tough week for the Stars as all three games were canceled due to an outbreak on the team of the coronavirus. Seventeen players have caught the virus and while the Stars re-opened their facility Tuesday and practiced, the hope is that they will be able to play this week…There has been no word on the re-scheduling of the three games they missed, two in Florida and one in Tampa Bay, but there was word Thursday that the Florida game on the 19th could be postponed as well. Stay tuned… The line of Jamie Benn, Roope Hintz and Denis Gurianov have looked great together in training camp as coach Rick Bowness said the trio have been dominating practice…Of course, we currently do not know who is at practice because so many Stars are ill…Thomas Harley joined the Stars on Wednesday after being their only prospect at the World Junior Championships...Anton Khudobin practiced for the first time on Tuesday as he had COVID-19 in Russia as well as having some immigration troubles before arriving in Dallas.

DETROIT

Games this week

vs. Columbus, vs. Columbus, @ Chicago, @ Chicago  

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Red Wings dropped a 3-0 decision to Carolina Thursday but received outstanding netminding from Thomas Greiss who turned aside 40-of-42 shots…The Red Wings should be better in net this season with Greiss replacing Jimmy Howard…Bobby Ryan was scratched Thursday as he is trying to recover from an undisclosed injury…Darren Helm is on the COVID-19 list…The Red Wings claimed defenseman Christian Djoos off waivers from Anaheim…Dylan Larkin has been named captain of the Detroit Red Wings, their first captain since 2018 when Henrik Zetterberg retired…Evgeny Svechnikov passed through waivers and is on the Red Wings taxi squad.

EDMONTON

Games this week

vs. Montreal, @ Toronto, @ Toronto, @ Winnipeg

Tired: None

Rested: @ Winnipeg (Sunday)

Notes

The Oilers split their two game opening series with Vancouver…Connor McDavid was held off the scoresheet in the opener but came back to pick up a hat trick and add an assist Thursday…Leon Draisaitl has five assists in his first two games…Mikko Koskinen started both games after Mike Smith was ruled unfit to play for Thursday’s contest…The Oilers called up Stuart Skinner to back up Koskinen…Tyler Ennis has a foot injury caused by blocking a shot in Wednesday’s game…Devin Shore took his spot on the fourth line Thursday…Evan Bouchard is on the taxi squad…James Neal is in COVID-19 protocol.  

FLORIDA

Games this week

vs. Chicago, @ Carolina, @ Carolina,  

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Panthers had their two-game series with Dallas postponed last week due to the outbreak of COVID-19 on the Stars and do not start their NHL season until Sunday the 17th…Florida sent outstanding rookie Grigori Denisenko to the minors. Denisenko is worth picking up once he is recalled…Markus Nutivaara is on the COVID-19 list…The Panthers signed defenseman Kevin Connauton to a one-year/two-way contract…Sergei Bobrovsky is starting the season as an injured/non-roster player but he could return for the opener on the 17th as he was back on the ice practicing Wednesday…Florida claimed former first round pick Noah Juulsen off waivers from Montreal.

LOS ANGELES

Games this week

vs. Colorado, vs. Colorado, @ St. Louis, @ St. Louis

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Kings play four games this week including three games in four nights…Jonathan Quick should get the start in two games with either Cal Petersen (who currently on the sidelines with COVID-19) or Jacob Ingham getting the other start... Quick gave up four goals on 27 shots in an overtime loss to Minnesota Thursday…Dustin Brown scored Thursday and it was his 300th goal of his NHL career…Kurtis MacDermid and Sean Walker are also in COVID-19 protocol…Second overall pick Quinten Byfield has been sent to the minors as has Arthur Kaliyev…Lias Andersson was drafted seventh overall in 2017 by the Rangers and traded to the Kings in the off-season. He will start the season on the taxi squad.

 

MINNESOTA

Games this week

@ Anaheim, @ Anaheim, vs. San Jose, vs. San Jose

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Wild complete their opening road trip to Los Angeles with a pair of games against Anaheim before returning home to face the Sharks…It was a big night for rookie Kirill Kaprizov who had the overtime winner and added a pair of assists in a 4-3 win over Los Angeles. He also led all forwards, playing 21:52…Marcus Foligno signed a three-year contract extension Tuesday…Kevin Fiala was held off the scoresheet Thursday…First-round pick Marco Rossi is out indefinitely with an upper-body injury…Matt Dumba had an assist in the opener and while his name has been bandied about in trade rumors, he wants to remain with the Wild.

MONTREAL

Games this week

@ Edmonton, @ Vancouver, @ Vancouver, @ Vancouver

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Canadiens play all four games on the road including three games in four nights in Vancouver…Expect to see Jake Allen at least once in net and possibly twice with Carey Price getting the remainder of the starts…Josh Anderson scored a pair against Toronto in his Montreal debut Wednesday while Jonathan Drouin had three assists…Corey Perry is starting the season on the taxi squad after he cleared waivers…Alexander Romanov was impressive in his NHL debut as the defenseman had an assist in 21:48 of action…Nick Suzuki looked great as well and if for some reason he is on your waiver wire, grab him now.

NASHVILLE

Games this week

vs. Carolina, vs. Carolina, @ Dallas, @ Dallas

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Predators used Juuse Saros over Pekka Rinne in the opener and that could be the way it goes this season as this could be Rinne’s last season in the NHL, Saros was outstanding, stopping 29-of-30 Columbus shots in a 3-1 win…This is a big season for Ryan Johansen as he had only 14 goals and 36 points last season…Mikael Granlund is on the NHL’s COVID Protocol Related Absence…Philip Tomasino was sent to the minors following his four goal, six point effort for Canada at the WJC…Eeli Tolvanen is starting the season on the taxi squad…The Predators claimed Luca Sbisa off waivers from Winnipeg…Erik Haula had an assist in his Nashville debut after missing part of training camp due to the birth of his child.

NEW JERSEY

Games this week

@ New York Rangers, @ New York Islanders, vs. New York Islanders

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Devils had to be surprised when their big free agent signing of the off-season, Corey Crawford, decided to retire before the start of the regular season…That means that Mackenzie Blackwood will be the undisputed number one goalie in New Jersey with Scott Wedgewood as the backup…Jack Hughes started the 2020-21 campaign off right with a pair of assists in a 3-2 shootout loss to Boston…Hughes is playing alongside Egor Sharangovich and Kyle Palmieri…Will Butcher, a fixture the last three seasons on the Devils blueline, was a healthy scratch Thursday…Nico Hischier missed Thursday’s opener with a leg injury…The Devils claimed Eric Comrie off waivers from Winnipeg and he will be the team’s third goaltender.

NEW YORK ISLANDERS

Games this week

Vs. Boston, vs. New Jersey, @ New Jersey

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Islanders signed Mathew Barzal to a three-year deal and the sound you heard from New York was a huge sign of relief…The Islanders looked great in their debut, shutting out the Rangers 4-0 behind Semyon Varlamov’s 24 saves…Anders Lee led the way with a goal and an assist…Oliver Wahlstrom and Kieffer Bellows were  placed on the taxi squad…The Islanders signed Cory Schneider to be their third goaltender…Ilya Sorokin will be the backup with the Islanders this season and he is aware that Islander fans are so excited about him. He should see plenty of action this season as the co-number one with Varlamov…The Islanders had some more official signings once Barzal inked his deal as Matt Martin signed a four-year deal for $6 million and Andy Greene signed on for one season at $700,000…

NEW YORK RANGERS

Games this week

vs. New Jersey, @ Pittsburgh, @ Pittsburgh

Tired: None

Rested: None

Notes

The Rangers were surprised in losing 4-0 to the Islanders and coach David Quinn was not happy with the performance as he thought that the team was not prepared to play…Alexis Lafreniere was obviously held pointless in his NHL debut…Mika Zibanejad missed the first week of camp as he tested positive for COVID-19, but was still in the starting lineup Thursday between Chris Kreider and Pavel Buchnevich… Braden Schneider, the Rangers first pick and 19th overall in 2020, signed an AHL only contract with Hartford. Schneider missed the December 31st deadline on late birthdays (after September 15) to sign an entry-level deal and this was the best option.

OTTAWA     

Games this week

vs. Winnipeg, vs. Winnipeg, @ Winnipeg

Tired: None

Rested: vs. Winnipeg (Tuesday)

Notes

The Senators play the Jets all three times this week with two at home and one on the road.
